Designing for Impact: Social Media for AHHF

Campaign-ready graphic design that mobilises community support, drives fundraising, and amplifies real-world action for a grassroots non-profit foundation.

Client

Approach Helping Hands Foundation

Timeline

2013 – Ongoing

Services

Social Media Design, Print Collateral

The Challenge

Approach Helping Hands Foundation operates on the ground — distributing essential kits, running fundraising drives, and coordinating volunteers between urban donors and rural communities. The problem was that their visual communication didn't match the scale or urgency of their work. Across Instagram, WhatsApp, and physical print materials, the foundation was relying on ad hoc graphics that lacked consistency, visual hierarchy, and the kind of emotional authority needed to move people to act.

The brief was clear but demanding: create a design system versatile enough to cover social posts, video forwards, fundraising flyers, branded notebooks, certificates, and fabric bags — all while maintaining coherence across wildly different formats and audiences. The work had to feel credible to a city-based donor scrolling Instagram and approachable to a rural volunteer receiving a WhatsApp forward.

Our Approach

We built a visual language anchored in warmth, clarity, and purpose. Rather than designing each campaign piece from scratch, we established a core template system — consistent color palette, typographic hierarchy, and iconographic style — that could be adapted quickly for new campaigns without losing identity. Every visual decision was made with two questions in mind: does this convey trust, and does this convey urgency?

For social media, we leaned into bold data visualisation paired with human photography, so that the impact of AHHF's work was immediately legible in the first frame of a scroll. For print, we designed for real-world production constraints — materials that could be printed locally, efficiently and locally, without sacrificing the professionalism that gives a non-profit its credibility. Campaigns for kit distribution, donation drives, and community events each received dedicated creative treatments, then fed back into the evolving template library.

The Result

AHHF now has a cohesive, campaign-ready design system that has been in continuous use since 2013. The visual identity has scaled across dozens of campaigns, multiple fundraising cycles, and a growing volunteer network — all without losing coherence. Social posts drive measurable engagement; print collateral reinforces the foundation's legitimacy at community events; and the template system means new campaign materials can be turned around rapidly whenever an urgent need arises.

More than just aesthetics, the design work has contributed directly to the foundation's ability to mobilise people — translating emotional storytelling and accessible data into real-world action: donations processed, kits delivered, volunteers coordinated.
