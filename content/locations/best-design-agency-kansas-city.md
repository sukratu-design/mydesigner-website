# Best Design Agency in Kansas City: 10 Studios and One Remote Contender

Kansas City has a design scene that punches above its weight class. The Crossroads Arts District has become one of the densest creative corridors between Chicago and Denver, and KCAI (Kansas City Art Institute) keeps feeding trained talent into local studios. From employee-owned advertising giants to two-person branding shops, the metro covers a wider range of agency types than most cities its size.

The economics are part of the appeal. KC agency rates run 10-15% below the national average and 20-40% below coastal cities. Google Fiber made Kansas City one of the most connected cities in the US early, which attracted startups and tech companies that now fuel steady design demand. The food and beverage industry — anchored by a BBQ scene that draws national attention — generates a constant stream of packaging and brand identity work.

But the same question applies here as anywhere: do you hire local, or go remote? This guide profiles 10 Kansas City agencies worth knowing, then breaks down the math on when a remote design subscription like MyDesigner makes more financial sense.

## Detailed Agency Profiles

### 1. BarkleyOKRP

[BarkleyOKRP](https://barkleyokrp.com) is the biggest name in Kansas City advertising. With 200+ employees and an employee-owned structure, they operate at a scale that most KC agencies cannot touch. Their client roster reads like a Fortune 500 shortlist. They handle brand strategy, creative campaigns, and media planning as integrated services.

**Specialty:** Full-service advertising, brand strategy, creative campaigns
**Best for:** Enterprise brands with six-figure-plus marketing budgets
**Typical cost:** $50,000-$200,000+ per engagement
**Tradeoff:** This is an enterprise shop. If your budget is under $50K or you need a quick logo refresh, you are not their target client. The employee-owned model means stability, but also means big-agency timelines and processes.

### 2. Willoughby Design

[Willoughby Design](https://willoughbydesign.com) is a premium brand strategy firm that has been shaping Kansas City's design reputation for decades. Their work on the KC Current stadium brand — naming, identity, the full system — demonstrates the level they operate at. They do brand strategy, identity, naming, and packaging, and they do it at a caliber that draws clients from outside Missouri.

**Specialty:** Brand strategy, identity systems, naming, packaging
**Best for:** Mid-to-enterprise companies investing in a serious brand build
**Typical cost:** $25,000-$100,000 per engagement
**Tradeoff:** Branding only. No web development, no ongoing design support, no social media production. You get a world-class brand system, then you need someone else to execute it day to day.

### 3. Design Ranch

[Design Ranch](https://design-ranch.com) is a women-owned studio of 2-10 people that specializes in lifestyle branding, packaging, and graphic design. Their sweet spot is food and beverage, arts organizations, and lifestyle brands — the kind of work that Kansas City's culture generates naturally. Small team means the founders are hands-on with every project.

**Specialty:** Lifestyle branding, packaging, graphic design
**Best for:** Food/drink brands, arts organizations, lifestyle companies
**Typical cost:** $10,000-$50,000 per project
**Tradeoff:** Small team with limited bandwidth. If you need fast turnaround on high-volume work, their capacity may not match your timeline. Lifestyle-focused — not the pick for enterprise SaaS or fintech.

### 4. REACTOR Design

[REACTOR Design](https://reactor.design) operates out of the Crossroads Arts District and brings a strategic approach to branding, identity, print, and packaging. They position themselves as problem-solvers for complex business challenges, not just visual designers. Demand is high enough that they occasionally run a waitlist.

**Specialty:** Strategic branding, identity, print, packaging
**Best for:** Companies with complex positioning challenges that need more than a pretty logo
**Typical cost:** $15,000-$75,000 per engagement
**Tradeoff:** The waitlist is real. If you need work started next week, REACTOR may not be available. Their strategic depth commands premium pricing relative to the KC market.

### 5. Lifted Logic

[Lifted Logic](https://liftedlogic.com) is a web-focused agency that builds custom websites, handles SEO, and produces photo and video content. Their rates range from $10K projects to $150K custom builds, with hourly rates of $200-$300/hr. They are one of the strongest web development shops in Kansas City.

**Specialty:** Custom web development, SEO, photo/video production
**Best for:** Businesses that need a lead-generating website built properly
**Typical cost:** $10,000-$150,000 per project, $200-$300/hr
**Tradeoff:** Web only. They do not do brand identity, packaging, or print design. If you need a logo before you need a website, start somewhere else and come back.

### 6. Crema

[Crema](https://crema.us) builds digital products — mobile apps, web applications, and software platforms. Their team combines UX/UI design with software development, which makes them a strong fit for startups and companies building custom technology products. They think in terms of products, not websites.

**Specialty:** Digital product design, UX/UI, software development
**Best for:** Startups and companies building apps or software platforms
**Typical cost:** $25,000-$150,000 per engagement
**Tradeoff:** Products, not websites. If you need a marketing site, a brand identity, or social media graphics, Crema is the wrong shop. Their focus is building things that users interact with daily.

### 7. Novella Brandhouse

[Novella Brandhouse](https://novellabrandhouse.com) is a women-owned boutique agency that handles brand strategy, identity, and marketing for scaling businesses. They have carved out a niche in B2G (business-to-government) and higher education markets — two sectors with specific communication requirements that generalist agencies often fumble.

**Specialty:** Brand strategy, identity, marketing
**Best for:** Scaling businesses, B2G contracts, higher education institutions
**Typical cost:** $10,000-$50,000 per engagement
**Tradeoff:** Boutique scale means selective client capacity. Their B2G and higher ed expertise is valuable if you are in those markets, but adds no premium if you are a DTC brand.

### 8. Honeystreet

[Honeystreet](https://honeystreet.com) combines branding with motion — animation, video, and identity work that moves. For brands that need more than static assets, Honeystreet fills a gap that most KC agencies leave open. Their work spans brand identity systems and animated content that brings those systems to life.

**Specialty:** Branding, animation, video, identity
**Best for:** Brands that need motion and identity working together
**Typical cost:** $5,000-$40,000 per project
**Tradeoff:** Not a web development firm. If you need a website built, you will need a second vendor. Their strength is visual identity and motion, not code.

### 9. Brittney Murphy Design

[Brittney Murphy Design](https://brittneymurphydesign.com) is a solo studio specializing in custom typography, web design, and brand identity. The custom typeface work is the differentiator — few agencies in Kansas City (or anywhere) offer bespoke type design as a core service. If your brand identity hinges on owning a unique typeface, this is the shop.

**Specialty:** Custom typography, web design, brand identity
**Best for:** Brands that want a custom typeface as the foundation of their identity
**Typical cost:** $2,000-$15,000 per project
**Tradeoff:** Solo studio with limited capacity. Large-scale projects or tight deadlines may exceed what one person can deliver. The niche focus is a strength if you need it, irrelevant if you do not.

### 10. Square Peg Marketing

[Square Peg Marketing](https://neverfitin.com) handles branding, web design, and digital marketing for small and mid-size businesses. Based in Olathe (Kansas side of the metro), they serve the broader KC area with a smaller team and more accessible pricing than the downtown agencies.

**Specialty:** Branding, web design, digital marketing
**Best for:** SMBs that need branding and web under one roof
**Typical cost:** $5,000-$25,000 per project
**Tradeoff:** Smaller team and suburban positioning. Companies looking for a Crossroads-district creative partner or enterprise-scale campaigns will want a different agency. But for small businesses that need solid execution without downtown overhead pricing, they deliver.

## How Kansas City Compares to Other US Design Markets

Kansas City is one of the best value design markets in the country. Agency rates run 10-15% below the national average and 20-40% below coastal cities like San Francisco, New York, or Los Angeles. A strong talent pipeline from KCAI (Kansas City Art Institute) keeps the quality high even as rates stay accessible.

Where Kansas City stands out: brand identity and packaging (driven by the food/beverage and consumer goods industries), advertising (BarkleyOKRP alone employs more creatives than some cities have total), and a growing startup ecosystem fueled by early Google Fiber infrastructure and organizations like KC Startup Village. Where the market is thinner: deep enterprise SaaS product design and the kind of high-volume UI/UX work that Austin or Minneapolis produce at scale.

The Crossroads Arts District has become a genuine creative hub — not just a neighborhood with a few studios, but a concentrated ecosystem where agencies, freelancers, galleries, and clients overlap. That density creates collaboration and competition that benefits buyers.

## When a Local Kansas City Agency Is the Wrong Choice

Skip the local agency search entirely if:

- **Your design volume exceeds 15 requests per month.** At project rates, you will spend more than a full-time salary equivalent. Subscription models are built for this volume.
- **You need three disciplines (UI + graphic + web) under one roof.** Most Kansas City studios specialize in one or two areas. Coordinating multiple vendors burns time and creates inconsistency.
- **Your budget is under $3,000/month but your needs are steady.** At Kansas City hourly rates, that buys you 10-40 hours of work depending on the agency. A subscription model gets you a full team for less.
- **Location truly does not matter for your business.** If you are a SaaS company selling to a national audience, Kansas City-specific market knowledge adds no value.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much should I budget for a design agency in Kansas City?**
For a one-time project like a brand identity or website, budget $5,000-$25,000 depending on complexity. Kansas City rates run 10-15% below the national average, making it one of the more accessible design markets in the US. For ongoing design work, expect to spend $3,000-$10,000/month at agency rates. If your monthly spend would consistently exceed $3,000, compare that against subscription services like MyDesigner where [$1,400-$3,800/mo](https://mydesigner.gg/#pricing) gets you a dedicated team.

**Can a remote design service replace a local Kansas City agency?**
For execution-heavy work (social graphics, landing pages, UI screens, pitch decks, email templates), yes. Remote subscriptions often deliver more output per dollar because the subscription model eliminates project-scoping overhead. For brand strategy tied to KC's food and beverage scene, local startup ecosystem, or Crossroads creative community, a Kansas City agency still has the edge.

**What is the fastest way to get design work done in Kansas City?**
Local agencies typically quote 1-3 week timelines for new projects, with discovery and onboarding adding more time upfront. Design subscriptions like MyDesigner start delivering within 24-48 hours of your first request because onboarding is streamlined and the team is already in place.
