Best Design Agency in Salt Lake City: 10 Studios and One Remote Contender

Salt Lake City has quietly built one of the most interesting design markets in the Mountain West. The Silicon Slopes tech corridor — stretching from SLC through Lehi and Provo — has created sustained demand for product design, UX/UI, and brand systems that serve fast-growing SaaS companies. Add in the outdoor recreation industry, a thriving biotech sector, and a cost of living that keeps agency overhead 5-8% below the national average, and you get a market that produces strong design talent at competitive prices.

The agencies here reflect that mix. You will find product design studios working with Meta and National Geographic alongside full-service shops serving Utah's small business ecosystem. The pricing advantage is real — SLC rates sit below what you will pay in Denver, Portland, or any coastal city for comparable work.

This guide profiles 10 Salt Lake City agencies you should evaluate, then breaks down when a remote design subscription makes more financial sense.

Detailed Agency Profiles

1. Underbelly

Underbelly is SLC's premium product design studio, working with Meta, National Geographic, and Starbucks. They handle product design, UX/UI, branding, web apps, and video production. This is the shop you call when the design itself is the product — not just a wrapper around it.

Specialty: Product design, UX/UI, branding, web apps, video production
Best for: Startups and enterprise brands needing product-grade design
Typical cost: Mid-to-premium range; projects typically $25,000-$100,000+
Tradeoff: Premium pricing puts them out of reach for small local businesses. You are paying for a client roster that includes household names.

2. Struck

Struck has spent 25+ years in Salt Lake City building brand identity, advertising strategy, web design, mobile apps, and retail design. Their sweet spot is destination marketing, outdoor recreation, and biotech — industries that define Utah's economy. Award-winning work and a deep understanding of the Mountain West market set them apart.

Specialty: Brand identity, advertising strategy, web design, mobile apps, retail design
Best for: Established brands in destination marketing, outdoor recreation, and biotech
Typical cost: Premium; projects typically $25,000-$75,000
Tradeoff: High minimums geared toward larger brand transformation projects. Not set up for quick, low-budget deliverables.

3. Hint Creative

Hint Creative builds brand systems and packaging design with a portfolio that includes Nike, Fender, American Eagle, and Harvard University. They are particularly strong in CPG, beauty, and wellness — industries where packaging is the first and often only touchpoint with the customer. Their brand systems are built to work across shelf, screen, and social simultaneously.

Specialty: Brand systems, packaging design, identity design for CPG, beauty, wellness
Best for: Startups and established brands in CPG, beauty, and wellness
Typical cost: Mid-to-premium range; projects typically $15,000-$50,000
Tradeoff: Focused on brand and packaging. Not a full-service digital shop — you will need a second vendor for web development or digital marketing.

4. ThoughtLab

Founded in 1999, ThoughtLab operates from SLC, San Francisco, New York, and Atlanta. They handle brand strategy, premium websites, marketing, and custom software development. Their focus is on category design — helping companies define or redefine the space they compete in, not just look better within it.

Specialty: Brand strategy, premium websites, marketing, custom software development
Best for: Companies wanting brand-first category design and differentiation
Typical cost: Mid-to-premium range; projects typically $20,000-$75,000
Tradeoff: Multi-city footprint means the SLC team may not always be the primary team on your project. Strategy-heavy approach adds timeline.

5. Modern8

Modern8 is a small SLC team (~10 people) focused on brand strategy, identity design, print, packaging, and web. Their client list includes the University of Utah and regional institutions. The small team size keeps overhead low and means you work directly with senior creatives — no account management layers between you and the people doing the work.

Specialty: Brand strategy, identity design, print, packaging, web
Best for: Mid-market businesses and institutions wanting strategic brand positioning
Typical cost: Mid-range; projects typically $10,000-$30,000
Tradeoff: Small team means limited bandwidth. Large-scale projects with tight deadlines may exceed their capacity.

6. Afton Klein Group

Afton Klein Group is a multi-disciplinary design and communications agency covering brand identity, positioning, website and digital experience, motion, and environments. They serve architecture, education, technology, and healthcare sectors — industries where design needs to communicate complex ideas clearly across multiple formats.

Specialty: Brand identity, positioning/strategy, website/digital experience, motion, environments
Best for: Architecture, education, technology, and healthcare organizations
Typical cost: Mid-to-premium range; projects typically $10,000-$40,000
Tradeoff: Boutique size means longer lead times. Industry focus is a strength for aligned clients but may not translate to consumer or DTC brands.

7. Big Red Jelly

Big Red Jelly handles branding, web design, and digital marketing as an integrated package — what they call the "brand-build-grow" cycle. Based in Provo (serving the greater SLC metro), they are rated on Clutch at $150-$199/hr with a low minimum project threshold of $1,000+, making them accessible to small and mid-size businesses across Utah.

Specialty: Branding, web design, digital marketing, automations
Best for: Small to mid-size businesses wanting the full brand-build-grow cycle
Typical cost: $150-$199/hr; minimum project $1,000+; projects typically $5,000-$25,000
Tradeoff: Based in Provo, not SLC proper. Generalist approach means less deep specialization in any single area.

8. Amply

Amply specializes exclusively in B2B Webflow design, development, and migrations. They have completed 150+ projects for clients including Dell and ZenHub. For Silicon Slopes SaaS companies that have standardized on Webflow, Amply offers deep platform expertise that generalist agencies cannot match.

Specialty: B2B Webflow design, development, migrations, conversion optimization
Best for: B2B and SaaS companies wanting Webflow-built marketing sites
Typical cost: Mid-to-premium range; projects typically $10,000-$40,000
Tradeoff: Webflow-only. If you need WordPress, Shopify, or a custom CMS, Amply is not the right fit.

9. Red Olive

Red Olive has operated in the SLC area for 22+ years as a Google Premier Partner (top 3%). They handle web design, SEO, PPC, and digital marketing with a focus on lead generation. For businesses where the website needs to produce measurable leads — not just look good — their performance marketing background is the differentiator.

Specialty: Web design, SEO, PPC, digital marketing, lead generation
Best for: Businesses focused on web presence and lead generation; local SEO-heavy businesses
Typical cost: Mid-range; projects typically $5,000-$20,000
Tradeoff: Heavier on marketing and SEO than pure design. If your primary need is brand identity or creative direction, their performance-first approach may not deliver the visual distinction you want.

10. 360 Elevated

360 Elevated is a veteran-owned full-service agency covering advertising, branding, graphic design, media buying, SEO, video production, and website development. Their client list includes the U.S. Air Force, Stanford, and Marriott. They offer flexible pricing — hourly, month-to-month, project-based, and recurring — which makes them accessible across budget levels.

Specialty: Advertising, branding, graphic design, media buying, SEO, video, web development
Best for: Government agencies, nonprofits, healthcare, and automotive
Typical cost: Competitive pricing with flexible models; projects typically $5,000-$25,000
Tradeoff: Based in Layton, UT (30 minutes north of SLC). Full-service generalist approach means less deep specialization in any single design discipline.

How Salt Lake City Compares to Other US Design Markets

Salt Lake City offers some of the best value in the US for design services. Designer salaries run 2-10% below the national average, and the city's lower cost of living (5-8% below the US average) keeps agency overhead competitive. You pay less than Denver, Portland, Austin, or Charlotte for comparable quality, while tapping into a talent pool shaped by Silicon Slopes tech demand.

Where SLC stands out is product design and UX/UI. The concentration of SaaS companies along the Wasatch Front has produced agencies with genuine product design depth — not just "we can do apps too" generalists. Where the market is thinner: luxury branding, consumer lifestyle brands, and the kind of craft-driven agency culture you find in Portland or Austin. SLC's design scene is practical and tech-forward, not aspirational.

When a Local SLC Agency Is the Wrong Choice

Skip the local agency search entirely if:

  • Your design volume exceeds 15 requests per month. At project rates, you will spend more than a full-time salary equivalent. Subscription models are built for this volume.
  • You need three disciplines (UI + graphic + web) under one roof. Most SLC studios specialize in one or two areas. Coordinating multiple vendors burns time and creates inconsistency.
  • Your budget is under $3,000/month but your needs are steady. At SLC hourly rates, that buys you 15-30 hours of work. A subscription model gets you a full team for less.
  • Location truly does not matter for your business. If you are a SaaS company selling to a national audience, Utah-specific market knowledge adds no value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I budget for a design agency in Salt Lake City? For a one-time project like a brand identity or website, budget $5,000-$25,000 depending on complexity. SLC rates are competitive — 2-10% below the national average. For ongoing design work, expect to spend $3,000-$10,000/month at agency rates. If your monthly spend would consistently exceed $3,000, compare that against subscription services like MyDesigner where $1,400-$3,800/mo gets you a dedicated team.

Can a remote design service replace a local SLC agency? For execution-heavy work (social graphics, landing pages, UI screens, pitch decks, email templates), yes. Remote subscriptions often deliver more output per dollar because the subscription model eliminates project-scoping overhead. For product design work that requires deep technical collaboration with your engineering team, or packaging design that needs physical production oversight, a local agency still has the edge.

What is the fastest way to get design work done in Salt Lake City? Local agencies typically quote 1-3 week timelines for new projects, with discovery and onboarding adding more time upfront.
