Best Design Agency in Nashville: 10 Studios and One Remote Contender

Nashville's design scene has grown well past the music industry logos and honky-tonk signage that once defined it. The city's creative corridor now stretches from the Gulch tech startups commissioning product UI to East Nashville DTC brands needing packaging and social content. Healthcare companies around the HCA corridor, hospitality ventures along Broadway, and a growing SaaS presence all keep local agencies busy.

But Nashville agency pricing creates a real decision point. Rates track close to the national average, which means you get solid talent without coastal markup — but ongoing design work at project rates still adds up fast. This guide profiles 10 Nashville agencies worth knowing, then breaks down when a remote design subscription makes more financial sense.

Detailed Agency Profiles

1. Huckleberry Branding

Huckleberry Branding covers the full identity spectrum: logo design, brand guidelines, graphic design, web design, and SEO. Their published pricing is unusually transparent for a Nashville agency — logo packages start at $4,500, style guides from $3,000. That openness signals confidence in their process and makes budgeting straightforward.

Specialty: Branding, logo design, graphic design, web design, SEO
Best for: Small to mid-size businesses building a brand identity from scratch
Typical cost: $5,000-$15,000 per engagement
Tradeoff: Full-service packaging means you pay for the whole suite even if you only need one piece. Not a pure web development shop.

2. Nice Branding Agency

Nice Branding Agency has carved out a niche that perfectly fits Nashville's hospitality-heavy economy. They run a dedicated restaurant branding division, which means they understand menu psychology, signage flow, and the kind of brand systems that need to work at 2 AM on Broadway as well as they do on Instagram.

Specialty: Brand strategy, brand identity, restaurant and hospitality branding
Best for: Restaurants, hotels, and food/beverage brands needing industry-specific expertise
Typical cost: $10,000-$50,000 per engagement ($150-$199/hr)
Tradeoff: Premium pricing and heavy hospitality focus. SaaS companies and tech startups may find the brand thinking skews toward physical experiences over digital products.

3. Proof Branding

With 15+ years in Nashville, Proof Branding brings deep expertise in brand strategy, logo design, product packaging, and content strategy. They are particularly strong with consumer product brands that need packaging to perform on shelves and screens simultaneously. Their longevity in Nashville's creative market speaks to repeat business and consistent delivery.

Specialty: Brand strategy, logo design, product packaging, web design, content strategy
Best for: Product brands, hospitality, and CPG companies needing packaging + identity
Typical cost: $10,000-$50,000+ per engagement ($150-$199/hr)
Tradeoff: Skews toward physical product branding. Pure digital or SaaS companies may find more relevant expertise at a tech-focused shop.

4. JLB

JLB has been voted #1 Nashville web design for 18 consecutive years. They handle web design, graphic design, SEO, and PPC under one roof. Their strength is the sustained local reputation — if you are a Nashville business that wants a partner who understands the Brentwood-to-Franklin corridor as well as they understand responsive design, JLB delivers that.

Specialty: Web design, graphic design, SEO, PPC, digital marketing
Best for: Local Nashville businesses and franchises needing ongoing web + marketing support
Typical cost: $100-$149/hr; projects typically $5,000-$25,000
Tradeoff: Generalist agency. Jack of all trades means branding depth may be thinner than a dedicated identity shop.

5. Horton Group

Horton Group operates at the intersection of design and digital marketing. They build websites, run SEO campaigns, produce content, and handle graphic design for Nashville businesses across healthcare, legal, and professional services. Their size means they can handle enterprise-scale projects that would overwhelm a boutique studio.

Specialty: Web design, SEO, content marketing, eCommerce, graphic design
Best for: Mid-market businesses and enterprises needing a full digital marketing + design partner
Typical cost: $100-$149/hr; minimum project size $10,000+
Tradeoff: Larger agency overhead. Better for bigger budgets — may be overkill for a simple logo or a one-off landing page.

6. 323 Design

Founded in 2003, 323 Design is a boutique Nashville firm that keeps things personal. Their graphic design and web work comes with the kind of hands-on attention that larger agencies cannot match. You talk to the people doing the work, not an account manager relaying feedback through three layers.

Specialty: Web design, branding, digital marketing, SEO
Best for: Small businesses wanting personal, hands-on creative service
Typical cost: $100-$149/hr; projects typically $5,000-$15,000
Tradeoff: Smaller team means limited capacity. If your project needs 5 designers working in parallel, this is not the right fit.

7. Quantum Branding

Quantum Branding positions itself as a "science-based branding" firm, applying research methodology to brand strategy. They explicitly target companies at $2M+ in revenue who are ready to invest seriously in brand equity. This is not a logo-in-a-week shop — it is a strategic engagement that reshapes how a company presents itself to the market.

Specialty: Brand strategy, brand identity, research-driven branding
Best for: Growth-stage companies ($2M+ revenue) investing seriously in brand equity
Typical cost: $25,000+ for brand strategy engagements
Tradeoff: High minimums and revenue requirements. Bootstrapped startups and small businesses are not their target client.

8. Duck.Design

Duck.Design operates on a subscription model — $1,199/mo for graphic design, $1,899/mo for design + UX/UI + motion, or $1,999/mo for no-code development. They handle UI/UX design, website redesign, branding, and motion design. The subscription approach makes them an interesting middle ground between traditional agencies and remote design services.

Specialty: UI/UX design, website redesign, branding, motion design, no-code development
Best for: Startups and tech companies that prefer monthly subscription pricing over project quotes
Typical cost: $1,199-$1,999/mo
Tradeoff: Remote-first, not Nashville-local for in-person meetings. Subscription means you are sharing designer time across their client roster.

9. Couch Creative Co.

Couch Creative Co. has built brands for 150+ companies, combining branding, graphic design, and photography into accessible packages. Their starting point of $2,500 makes them one of Nashville's most budget-friendly options for startups and solopreneurs who need professional brand work without enterprise-level investment.

Specialty: Branding, graphic design, photography, creative direction
Best for: Startups and solopreneurs on tighter budgets who still want quality work
Typical cost: Starting at $2,500 per project
Tradeoff: Lower price point means less strategic depth. Photography-heavy, with limited technical web development capability.

10. UPQODE

UPQODE combines web development with UI/UX design and technical SEO. They build sites engineered to load fast and rank well — not just look good in a Figma mockup. With strong Clutch reviews highlighting professionalism and on-time delivery, they are a practical choice for businesses that measure website success by traffic and conversions, not just aesthetics.

Specialty: Web development, UI/UX design, SEO, eCommerce
Best for: Businesses that need a website built for performance and organic search
Typical cost: $5,000-$20,000 per project ($100-$149/hr)
Tradeoff: Web-focused and WordPress-centric. If your primary need is print collateral or brand identity, this is not the right shop.

How Nashville Compares to Other US Design Markets

Nashville's design costs sit right at the national average — designer salaries run within 1-3% of the US median. Agency hourly rates of $100-$175 are meaningfully cheaper than New York ($150-$300/hr) or San Francisco ($175-$350/hr) but comparable to peer cities like Austin, Charlotte, and Raleigh.

Where Nashville stands out is industry depth. The city's concentration of healthcare companies (HCA, Community Health Systems), hospitality brands, and music/entertainment businesses has produced agencies with uncommon expertise in those verticals. If your business operates in healthcare or hospitality, Nashville agencies bring sector knowledge that coastal generalists cannot match. For pure SaaS or tech product design, the local talent pool is thinner.

When a Local Nashville Agency Is the Wrong Choice

Skip the local agency search entirely if:

  • Your design volume exceeds 15 requests per month. At project rates, you will spend more than a full-time salary equivalent. Subscription models are built for this volume.
  • You need three disciplines (UI + graphic + web) under one roof. Most Nashville studios specialize in one or two areas. Coordinating multiple vendors burns time and creates inconsistency.
  • Your budget is under $3,000/month but your needs are steady. At Nashville hourly rates, that buys you 17-30 hours of work. A subscription model gets you a full team for less.
  • Location truly does not matter for your business. If you are a SaaS company selling to a national audience, Nashville-specific market knowledge adds no value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I budget for a design agency in Nashville? For a one-time project like a brand identity or website, budget $5,000-$25,000 depending on complexity. For ongoing design work, expect to spend $3,000-$10,000/month at agency rates. If your monthly spend would consistently exceed $3,000, compare that against subscription services like MyDesigner where $1,400-$3,800/mo gets you a dedicated team.

Can a remote design service replace a local Nashville agency? For execution-heavy work (social graphics, landing pages, UI screens, pitch decks, email templates), yes. Remote subscriptions often deliver more output per dollar because the subscription model eliminates project-scoping overhead. For strategy-intensive work that benefits from Nashville market knowledge or in-person collaboration — especially in hospitality and healthcare — a local agency still has the edge.

What is the fastest way to get design work done in Nashville? Local agencies typically quote 1-3 week timelines for new projects, with discovery and onboarding adding more time upfront.
