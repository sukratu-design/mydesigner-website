Best Design Agency in Indianapolis: 10 Studios and One Remote Contender

Indianapolis punches above its weight in design. The city that hosts the Indianapolis Motor Speedway and a Salesforce hub of 4,000+ employees has quietly built one of the Midwest's most interesting creative markets. Mass Ave arts district houses a growing cluster of design studios. Broad Ripple feeds a steady stream of freelance talent. And a growing SaaS scene — anchored by companies like Salesforce, Lessonly (now Seismic), and High Alpha's portfolio — has pushed local agencies to sharpen their digital product design skills.

The economics help too. Indianapolis sits roughly 10% below the national average on cost of living, and designer salaries run about 5% below the national median. That means agencies here charge less than their counterparts in Chicago, Denver, or the coasts — while drawing from the same talent pipeline that serves the city's enterprise clients.

This guide profiles 10 Indianapolis agencies you should evaluate, then breaks down when a remote design subscription makes more financial sense.

Detailed Agency Profiles

1. Young & Laramore

Young & Laramore is Indianapolis's legacy advertising agency — a full-service shop that has run national campaigns for brands across the Midwest and beyond. They handle strategic marketing, brand positioning, and creative direction at an enterprise level. This is the agency brands call when they need a national TV spot or a category-defining campaign, not a website refresh.

Specialty: Full-service advertising, strategic marketing, national campaigns
Best for: Established brands investing in large-scale advertising and market positioning
Typical cost: Enterprise pricing — campaigns run well into six figures
Tradeoff: This is an ad agency, not a design studio. If you need a logo, a landing page, or ongoing graphic design, you are paying advertising overhead for execution work.

2. TBH Creative

TBH Creative combines strategic web design with digital marketing. They focus on mid-size businesses that need a website built around conversion goals, not just aesthetics. Their process is research-driven — they scope before they design, which means thorough discovery but longer timelines.

Specialty: Strategic web design, digital marketing, conversion-focused builds
Best for: Mid-size businesses investing in a website tied to measurable business goals
Typical cost: $150-199/hr with a $10,000 minimum project size
Tradeoff: Premium hourly rates and no standard packages. If you need quick design execution or ongoing production work, the project-based model is expensive for volume.

3. Contrast & Co.

Contrast & Co. handles brand strategy, identity design, and UX/UI as integrated services. They are the Indianapolis agency you call when the brand needs to be rebuilt from the ground up — not just a new logo, but a full strategic repositioning with the visual system to match. Their work is thorough and their rates reflect it.

Specialty: Brand strategy, identity systems, UX/UI design
Best for: Companies undergoing complete brand transformation
Typical cost: $150-199/hr
Tradeoff: Best suited for big branding projects. If you need ongoing graphic design production or a simple website, you are overpaying for their strategic capability.

4. The Basement

The Basement is an integrated advertising agency covering digital strategy, video production, and creative campaigns. With 51-200 employees, they operate at a scale that serves mid-to-large companies needing a full creative production pipeline. They produce everything from brand campaigns to video content to digital experiences.

Specialty: Integrated advertising, digital strategy, video production
Best for: Mid-to-large companies needing multi-channel creative production
Typical cost: Full-service agency pricing with large-agency overhead
Tradeoff: At 51-200 employees, you are paying for infrastructure. Small businesses and startups will find leaner options on this list.

5. Boldthink Creative

Boldthink Creative has carved out a specific niche: branding, marketing, and web design for logistics companies, manufacturers, and B2B firms. They understand the visual language of industrial sectors and build brands that work in boardrooms, trade shows, and equipment catalogs. Their $70-150/hr rates are competitive for the depth of industry knowledge they bring.

Specialty: Branding, marketing, web design for industrial and B2B sectors
Best for: Logistics, manufacturing, and B2B companies
Typical cost: $70-150/hr
Tradeoff: Niche industrial focus. If you are a consumer brand, SaaS product, or creative company, their portfolio and instincts are tuned to a different audience.

6. Second Street Creative

Second Street Creative is a boutique studio covering logo and identity design, web design, UX, and packaging. They keep their client list tight, which means you get hands-on creative attention from senior people rather than being passed to a junior designer after the pitch meeting. Good fit for SMBs that want a real creative partner, not a production line.

Specialty: Logo/identity design, web design, UX, packaging
Best for: SMBs wanting boutique creative with direct access to senior talent
Typical cost: Boutique pricing
Tradeoff: Small shop means limited bandwidth. If you need fast turnaround on high-volume work, capacity constraints will slow you down.

7. Sharp Guys Web Design

Sharp Guys Web Design focuses on web design, SEO, and digital marketing for SMBs in the $5M-$100M revenue range. Based in Fishers (just north of Indianapolis), they position themselves as the go-to web partner for established small businesses that need a professional online presence backed by search visibility.

Specialty: Web design, SEO, digital marketing
Best for: SMBs ($5M-$100M revenue) that need web + SEO under one roof
Typical cost: SEO packages from $2,100/mo
Tradeoff: Web only. No branding, no graphic design production, no UI/UX product design. If you need anything beyond a website and search marketing, you will need a second vendor.

8. Ayokay

Ayokay handles web design, development, and marketing for small businesses and nonprofits. With a team of about 10 people, they deliver a personal touch that larger agencies cannot match. They are a solid pick for organizations that value relationship-driven creative work over transactional project delivery.

Specialty: Web design, development, marketing
Best for: Small businesses and nonprofits wanting a hands-on creative partner
Typical cost: Boutique pricing
Tradeoff: Limited capacity at 10 people. If your design needs scale beyond their bandwidth, timelines stretch. Not built for high-volume production work.

9. Moonflower Marketing

Moonflower Marketing leads with StoryBrand messaging — the idea that your brand message needs to be clear before any design work begins. They then execute on WordPress websites and logo design. If your business has a messaging problem disguised as a design problem, their approach makes sense.

Specialty: StoryBrand messaging, WordPress websites, logo design
Best for: Businesses that need to fix their messaging before investing in design
Typical cost: Mid-range pricing
Tradeoff: Messaging-first, WordPress only. If you need custom UI/UX, Webflow, or design work beyond WordPress templates, this is not the right fit.

10. Lackey Designs

Lackey Designs offers affordable web design, SEO, PPC, and branding for budget-conscious small businesses. Their pricing starts at $200/month or performance-based models, which is among the lowest in the Indianapolis market. They position themselves as the accessible option for businesses that cannot justify agency-tier budgets.

Specialty: Affordable web design, SEO, PPC, branding
Best for: Budget-conscious small businesses getting started with design
Typical cost: From $200/mo or performance-based pricing
Tradeoff: You get what you pay for. At these price points, expect template-based work and limited creative customization. Businesses with serious design needs will outgrow this quickly.

How Indianapolis Compares to Other US Design Markets

Indianapolis is one of the most affordable design markets on this list. Designer salaries run about 5% below the national average, and the city's 10% lower cost of living translates into agency rates that undercut Chicago, Denver, and every coastal city by a meaningful margin. You get Midwest pricing with access to a talent pool shaped by the city's Salesforce ecosystem, a growing SaaS startup scene, and the kind of manufacturing and logistics economy that keeps B2B branding agencies sharp.

Where Indianapolis stands out: B2B and industrial branding (driven by the logistics and manufacturing base), SaaS product design (thanks to the Salesforce hub and High Alpha portfolio companies), and sports and event marketing (the Motor Speedway and NCAA headquarters create steady demand). Where the market is thinner: consumer DTC brands, luxury identity work, and the kind of deep enterprise UX/UI that Seattle or Boston produce in volume.

For companies headquartered in Indianapolis or the surrounding metro (Carmel, Fishers, Zionsville), the local agency advantage is real. For distributed teams that happen to be incorporated in Indiana, that advantage evaporates.

When a Local Indianapolis Agency Is the Wrong Choice

Skip the local agency search entirely if:

  • Your design volume exceeds 15 requests per month. At project rates, you will spend more than a full-time salary equivalent. Subscription models are built for this volume.
  • You need three disciplines (UI + graphic + web) under one roof. Most Indianapolis studios specialize in one or two areas. Coordinating multiple vendors burns time and creates inconsistency.
  • Your budget is under $3,000/month but your needs are steady. At Indianapolis hourly rates, that buys you 15-42 hours of work. A subscription model gets you a full team for less.
  • Location truly does not matter for your business. If you are a SaaS company selling to a national audience, Indianapolis-specific market knowledge adds no value.

Frequently Asked Questions

Indianapolis rates run below the national average thanks to the city's affordable Midwest cost structure. For ongoing design work, expect to spend $3,000-$10,000/month at agency rates. For strategy-intensive work that benefits from local market knowledge — like B2B branding for the logistics corridor or sports marketing tied to the Motor Speedway — a local agency still has the edge.
