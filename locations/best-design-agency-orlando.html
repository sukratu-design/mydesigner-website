Best Design Agency in Orlando: 10 Studios and One Remote Contender

Orlando's design market is shaped by forces most people do not associate with the city. Yes, the theme parks are here — Disney, Universal, SeaWorld — and the hospitality industry generates enormous demand for experiential design, wayfinding, and consumer-facing branding. But the real story is the I-4 corridor's diversification into simulation and defense tech, gaming studios, a growing healthcare cluster around Lake Nona Medical City, and a startup scene that has quietly expanded beyond tourism-adjacent businesses.

The pricing reflects a cost-of-living advantage that Florida metros share. Orlando designer salaries run roughly 6-7% below the national average — about $59,000/yr — which keeps agency overhead lower than coastal metros. Hourly rates span from $25 to nearly $200/hr, with a wide range of studios serving everything from local restaurants to defense contractors.

This guide profiles 10 Orlando agencies you should evaluate, then breaks down when a remote design subscription makes more financial sense.

Detailed Agency Profiles

1. REMIXED

REMIXED runs a subscription-based branding and marketing model out of Orlando. Instead of quoting per-project, they charge a flat monthly fee — roughly $7,800/mo — that covers branding, marketing strategy, content, and design execution. The model is unusual for a local agency and appeals to businesses that want ongoing output without the overhead of scoping every deliverable.

Specialty: Branding, marketing strategy, content, design — all on subscription
Best for: Companies wanting a bundled brand + marketing retainer from a local team
Typical cost: ~$7,800/mo subscription
Tradeoff: High monthly commitment for the Orlando market. If your design needs are intermittent or project-based, the subscription model may not pencil out. Also, you are paying for marketing services bundled with design — wasteful if you only need design.

2. Maven Creative

Maven Creative has spent 15 years building brands in Orlando — strategy, naming, identity systems, and brand guidelines. They are the agency to call when you need more than a logo: a positioning framework, a naming architecture, and a visual system that holds together across touchpoints. Their longevity in a market that churns agencies says something about the quality of their strategic work.

Specialty: Brand strategy, naming, identity systems
Best for: Companies investing in brand foundations — naming, positioning, visual identity
Typical cost: $10,000-$30,000 for brand strategy engagements
Tradeoff: Strategy-heavy. If you already have a brand and just need execution — social graphics, landing pages, email templates — you are overpaying for their discovery process.

3. PH3 Agency

PH3 Agency covers branding, web design, and digital marketing. They sit in Orlando's mid-market sweet spot — project minimums around $5,000-$10,000, hourly rates in the $100-$149/hr range. For businesses that need brand identity work combined with a web presence and some ongoing digital support, PH3 packages those services without the overhead of the city's enterprise-tier shops.

Specialty: Branding, web design, digital marketing
Best for: Mid-market companies needing brand + web + digital in one shop
Typical cost: $100-$149/hr; projects $5,000-$10,000
Tradeoff: Jack-of-all-trades positioning. Companies with deep needs in any single discipline — say, complex UX or high-end brand strategy — may find the work competent but not exceptional.

4. Camber Creative

Camber Creative builds digital products — mobile apps, web applications, and UX-heavy platforms. They are Orlando's go-to for projects where design serves engineering, not the other way around. At $150-$199/hr with $50,000+ project minimums, they work with funded startups and established companies that need to ship real software, not just marketing sites.

Specialty: Digital product design, UX/UI, mobile and web app development
Best for: Funded startups and enterprises building custom digital products
Typical cost: $150-$199/hr; projects $50,000+
Tradeoff: Premium tier. Overkill if you need a marketing website or brand refresh. Their model is built for product builds, not ongoing design production.

5. Appleton Creative

Appleton Creative has been running since 1987 — nearly four decades in Orlando's market. They handle everything: branding, web design, advertising, PR, video production, and media buying. The full-service model means they can own your entire marketing pipeline, which matters for larger Orlando businesses that want one vendor managing everything from billboard creative to website redesigns. Minimum engagement starts at $15,000.

Specialty: Full-service marketing — branding, web, advertising, PR, video, media buying
Best for: Established Orlando businesses wanting a single full-service partner
Typical cost: Projects starting at $15,000+
Tradeoff: Full-service means you are paying for services you may not need. If you just need design work, half of Appleton's capabilities — media buying, PR, video production — are overhead in your engagement price.

6. Juice Agency

Juice Agency specializes in branding, Webflow development, and custom illustration. Their work has earned Webflow awards, and their illustration-heavy style gives clients a distinctive visual identity that stock photography and templated design cannot replicate. For Orlando companies that want to stand out visually — particularly in the creative, hospitality, and entertainment sectors — Juice delivers real craft.

Specialty: Branding, Webflow development, custom illustration
Best for: Brands wanting distinctive visual identity with custom illustration and Webflow builds
Typical cost: $10,000-$40,000 for brand + web projects
Tradeoff: Illustration-forward aesthetic is distinctive but opinionated. If your brand needs a clean, corporate look rather than bold illustration, their signature style may not align. Webflow-only on the development side limits platform flexibility.

7. Toucan Design

Toucan Design has been in Orlando since 1991 — over three decades serving local businesses with graphic design, WordPress websites, and print collateral. They are a practical choice for Orlando businesses that still need print — brochures, trade show materials, menus, signage — alongside a basic web presence. Longevity in this market speaks to client retention and reliable delivery.

Specialty: Graphic design, WordPress websites, print design
Best for: Small businesses needing print + web from a long-established local studio
Typical cost: $3,000-$15,000 for web + print projects
Tradeoff: Traditional approach. If you need modern UI/UX, animation, or Webflow/Framer development, Toucan's strengths lie elsewhere. Best for straightforward WordPress sites and print-heavy work.

8. DuoGeeks

DuoGeeks claims over 5,000 websites built — a volume play that signals efficiency and process. They handle WordPress development, WooCommerce, and general web design for small to mid-size businesses. The volume speaks to a systemized approach: templated workflows, repeatable processes, and fast turnaround for standard business websites and e-commerce stores.

Specialty: WordPress, WooCommerce, web design at scale
Best for: Small to mid-size businesses wanting WordPress/WooCommerce sites with fast delivery
Typical cost: $5,000-$25,000 for web projects
Tradeoff: Volume-driven approach means less bespoke design. If you want a hand-crafted, one-of-a-kind web experience, their templated process is not built for that. WordPress-only limits platform options.

9. Galaxy Web Studios

Galaxy Web Studios offers web and mobile development at Orlando's most accessible price point — $25-$49/hr with projects starting at $1,000. They serve the budget tier of the market: small businesses, solopreneurs, and startups that need a functional web or mobile presence without committing $10,000+ upfront. The tradeoff is straightforward — lower rates mean less design sophistication.

Specialty: Web development, mobile development
Best for: Budget-conscious small businesses and startups needing basic web/mobile
Typical cost: $25-$49/hr; projects starting $1,000+
Tradeoff: Budget pricing comes with budget-tier design. For companies that need polished UI/UX or distinctive branding, the quality gap is real. Best for functional builds, not visual excellence.

10. Krotov Studio

Krotov Studio builds WordPress sites with transparent, published pricing — $1,700 to $4,000 depending on complexity. In a market where most agencies make you schedule a call before revealing costs, Krotov's upfront pricing is refreshing. They serve small businesses and solopreneurs who want to know exactly what they are paying before the first conversation. The range covers basic business sites through more customized WordPress builds.

Specialty: WordPress development with transparent published pricing
Best for: Small businesses wanting predictable costs and no pricing surprises
Typical cost: $1,700-$4,000 (published rates)
Tradeoff: WordPress-only and limited to standard business websites. No branding, no UX research, no custom application development. Good for what it does, but narrow in scope.

How Orlando Compares to Other US Design Markets

Orlando's agency pricing sits below the national average but above the cheapest US markets. Hourly rates range from $25 to $199/hr, with most agencies clustering in the $75-$150/hr range. Designer salaries average around $59,000/yr, roughly 6-7% below the national average — a gap that translates directly into lower agency rates compared to Miami, Austin, or West Coast cities.

Where Orlando stands out: experiential and hospitality design (theme parks create a talent pipeline), simulation and defense-adjacent UI/UX work, and a growing pool of Webflow and WordPress specialists. Where the market is thinner: enterprise SaaS product design, luxury branding, and deep conversion optimization. Orlando's creative community is practical and production-oriented, shaped by an economy that values execution speed — Disney and Universal do not wait for agencies to find themselves.

When a Local Orlando Agency Is the Wrong Choice

Skip the local agency search entirely if:

  • Your design volume exceeds 15 requests per month. At project rates, you will spend more than a full-time salary equivalent. Subscription models are built for this volume.
  • You need three disciplines (UI + graphic + web) under one roof. Most Orlando studios specialize in one or two areas. Coordinating multiple vendors burns time and creates inconsistency.
  • Your budget is under $3,000/month but your needs are steady. At Orlando hourly rates, that buys you 15-40 hours of work. A subscription model gets you a full team for less.
  • Location truly does not matter for your business. If you are a SaaS company, a defense tech startup, or a gaming studio selling to a national audience, Orlando-specific market knowledge adds no value to your design output.
